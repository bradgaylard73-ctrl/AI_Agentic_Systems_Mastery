The Wallace Line marks the boundary between Asian and Australasian fauna, running between Bali and Lombok and between Borneo and Sulawesi. It reflects deep ocean trenches that prevented land crossing even during Ice Ages. The Weber Line (further east) represents the faunal equilibrium point between Asian and Australasian species, while the Lydekker Line marks the boundary of the Australasian continental shelf. Together, these boundaries explain Indonesia’s unique biodiversity and evolutionary patterns.
