The Digital Presence Integrity Framework (DPIF) is a governance standard for AI-mediated representations of real people (DRRPs). It addresses Presence Drift — the erosion of identity fidelity, authority boundaries, consent integrity, and accountability when digital representations operate without controls. DPIF defines 12 Critical Presence Controls (CPCs) and 4 Supporting Presence Controls (SPCs) across seven categories: Identity, Authority, Consent, Disclosure, Context Risk, Semantic Integrity, and Containment. CPC failure is binary and non-compensatory. The framework operates at deployment level, uses evidence-based assessment, classifies deployments into four context risk tiers (Low, Moderate, High, Regulated), and governs the full deployment lifecycle from provisioning through archival. It also addresses inter-deployment conflicts and posthumous/incapacitated principal governance.
